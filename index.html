Synapse'22

Synapse is a state-level, time-bound competitive event to address real-life problems and social issues where the participants collaborate to build a prototype for a specific problem. This event will bring together the brightest innovators with a passion to create a meaningful change. Participants can choose to make a solution for a particular problem statement in a domain, and will be expected to pitch their solution to the jury at the end. This will be a wonderful opportunity to learn, grow and network for all students. At the end of the 24- hour hackathon, teams will be deploying their app and presenting it to the judges.

Problem Statements

PPT Template Register Here!
  1.   The Sacred Eye

I'm sure we've all observed people breaking the law and engaging in unruly behavior in public, and when we saw these incidents, we all wanted to do something about it, right? So get ready, guys; the time has come. Create a product, using concepts of Computer Vision, that tracks these activities in real time and records the information. As soon as such suspicion is raised, make sure the appropriate authorities are alerted.

Consider a school as an example. The module must focus on preventing students from fighting, running through the hallways, and other issues. The school officials must be informed if any such actions are recorded.

 2.   Mathworks

All the solutions to mathworks problem statements have to be in matlab.

IOT/ Embedded:
Design an emergency alert app using the microphone and accelerometer signal data from your mobile phone. It should be able to determine the degree of emergency (minor, major, critical) according to the changes in microphone signal amplitude as well as the motion of the mobile phone.

AI/ML:
Build an app that can recommend a similar movie (ex. Posters from similar genre) based on a user provided movie poster.

IOT:
Design an ECG monitoring system that can filter and send data to ThingSpeak (loT platform). When detecting an anomaly, alerts are to be generated and sent to any concerned person.

Communications:
Design an antenna to optimize transmission and reception in indoor environment.

 3.   Let's go Phishing!

Hacking and cracking can be done in many ways. Software bugs and vulnerabilities can be patched very quickly if found, But this is not true for social Engineering attacks. One of the most common attacks in social engineering is phishing. The young generation have sound knowledge on such attacks, but there do exist individuals who unfortunately fall for this trap. How would we want to help these people? One such way is to detect many such phishing attacks that come through mail or whatsapp using a combination of Artificial intelligence and a tad bit of human intervention.

Your task is to create an AI model that could detect such phishing attacks and notify the user about the possibility of such an attack.

  4.  Armed and ready?

A Mobile Manipulator is typically employed in a retail shop for logistics. Suppose you are given a shopping list of items belonging to different aisles that must be delivered to the customer. Your robot must navigate to the correct aisle, detect the object of interest, pick it off the shelf and drop it in the customer’s cart. How many of these tasks can you successfully complete? Get creative with how your robot looks and operates, your technique as well as the simulated objects and the Gazebo world!

You must use the following objects in your world. Bowl, Rag Doll, Plastic Cup, Cricket Ball, Coke Can. Feel free to use as many more as you like. How would you solve the challenge of big variations in shapes and sizes of objects? And will your solution work for items placed in different orientations? The more real life challenges you solve, the higher you’ll be graded!

  5.   Chronicles of cosmos

Space is fascinating yet terrifying. The sheer amount of galaxies and stars untravelled is unimaginable. Space agencies have sent numerous satellites to investigate and solve the mystery of space. Yet, space exploration is still the domain of a select few, based on their skill sets, celebrity status or bank balances. Planetary events that are striking when viewed on a monitor become absolutely spectacular when witnessed in VR.

Participants are expected to demonstrate to us a render of the chosen objects and will have to ensure that your end product is immersive and can be interacted with. Participants could model electronics used in satellites/rovers or demonstrate an explanation of communication principles used. Let your creativity drive your thoughts. Fascinate us with your creativity and capture the beauty of space in your AR/VR experience.

  6.   Automated Traffic Control

TIME IS MONEY. People are wasting vital time by lingering in front of red lights for 120 seconds when there is no traffic on the opposite side of the street.

To counter this issue, you are expected to create a traffic controller that uses embedded technology to sense the volume of traffic and determine the amount of time needed for either side of a junction. Consider the unusual circumstances of a muleteer, emergency vehicles, or VIP convoy waiting at the signal.
You are required to develop an intelligent traffic control system utilizing Deep Learning, IoT and Computer Vision technologies. The solution must be able to handle changing traffic conditions and respond appropriately.

  7.   Cipher

In a world where everything is digitized,paper work is still being managed in India largely in the physical form, it is tedious as they take up space to store, keeping important documents safe is a security concern and protecting them from being counterfeited is the biggest risk.

Using the technology of blockchain, provide a working solution to store documents on chain, they should be accessed when required and should be tamper resistant.

FAQs

Curious? Looking for answers?

When And Where Is The Hackathon Going To Be Held?

November 26-27th 2022, Pesu52, PES University RR Campus

Is There A Registration Fee For The Hackathon?

To be declared.

Do I Need A Team? What Is The Team Size For The Hackathon?

Yes, teams will be of sizes 3 and 4 members.

Do I Need To Have Any Prerequisites? Do I Require Any Skills?

Not at all, hackathons are the best way to learn and implement. Nevertheless, prior coding and domain knowledge is an added advantage.

Is The Event Open To Everyone?

Definitely! All students of Karnataka across all branches of Engineering are welcome to participate.

How Many Rounds Do I Have To Go Through?

There will be a preliminary round before the hackathon where participants will be asked to submit their ideas. We will pick 35 teams and they will be expected to make a working model of their idea on the day of the Hackthon.
